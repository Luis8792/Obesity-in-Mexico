Fight against overweight and obesity

By Luis Gael Rojo Tejo

Reason

Mexico is the second OECD country with the highest percentage of out-of-pocket spending on health.
Out-of-pocket spending on chronic diseases can condition a family to fall into poverty.
While the out-of-pocket expenditure of Mexicans is one of the highest in the world, the budget of the Ministry of Health has decreased more than 20% in real terms during the six-year term.
If an average family had to pay in full for diabetes treatment, they would have to go into debt and / or sell their assets.
In the most conservative scenario, households affected by a chronic illness could have an out-of-pocket expense of 20%.
In this case, income losses could reach 1% of GDP. The income lost by households affected by chronic diseases, projected to 2030, is equivalent to 7.3 times the UNAM budget.
